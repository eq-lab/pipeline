# For lenders

Pipeline is a credit facility that finances vetted commodity trade deals. Pipeline pays the senior coupon and T-bill accrual to on-chain lenders. Lending is permissioned — every lender passes KYC/KYB checks and Chainalysis screening before he may deposit.

<div class="callout info">
  <h4>Minimum deposit</h4>
  <p>The minimum deposit is <strong>$1,000 USDC</strong>. There is no maximum at the protocol level, though individual allocations may be capped during subscription windows.</p>
</div>

## Where yield comes from

Two sources. The first is the senior coupon paid by borrowers on trade-finance loans. The second is T-bill accrual on the USYC reserves that back undrawn PLUSD. Read the full mechanics at [How it works — yield engines](/how-it-works/yield-engines/).

## What can go wrong

Your capital is exposed to loan defaults, liquidity delays at the withdrawal queue, custodian operational failure, smart-contract risk, governance failure, regulatory action, and operator error. Each of these has a dedicated page explaining the mitigation and the residual risk you carry. 

Read the full breakdown at [Risks](/risks/) and [Defaults and losses](/defaults-and-losses/).
